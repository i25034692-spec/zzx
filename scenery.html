Yalong Bay

Known as "the best bay in China," Yalong Bay stretches 7.5 kilometers of powdery white sand along crystal-clear turquoise waters. Framed by lush green mountains and dotted with world-class luxury resorts, it is the crown jewel of Sanya's coastline. The bay's calm, warm waters maintain an average temperature of 25 degrees year-round, making it perfect for swimming, snorkeling, and water sports. The surrounding coral reef ecosystem teems with colorful marine life, while the nearby Yalong Bay Tropical Paradise Forest Park offers hiking trails with stunning panoramic ocean views from mountain-top observation platforms.

02

Wuzhi Mountain

Rising 1,867 meters above sea level, Wuzhi Mountain (Five-Finger Mountain) is the highest peak in Hainan and a sacred symbol of the indigenous Li people. The mountain's five peaks resemble a giant hand reaching toward the sky, giving it its name. Trekking through its pristine tropical rainforest reveals cascading waterfalls, rare orchids, misty bamboo groves, and panoramic views stretching all the way to the South China Sea on clear days. The mountain is home to the Hainan Gibbon, one of the world's rarest primates, as well as countless endemic plant species found nowhere else on Earth.

03

Wuzhizhou Island

A heart-shaped volcanic island located just off the coast of Sanya, Wuzhizhou Island is renowned as China's premier diving and marine recreation destination. The island's surrounding coral reef ecosystem supports over 2,700 species of marine life, including sea turtles, reef sharks, and thousands of tropical fish species. Underwater visibility often exceeds 20 meters, making it ideal for scuba diving and snorkeling. Above water, the island features dramatic volcanic rock formations, lush tropical gardens with over 2,000 plant species, and a wedding plaza perched on dramatic cliff edges overlooking the boundless ocean.

Yanoda: Adventure in the treetops
04

Yanoda Rainforest

Yanoda Rainforest Cultural Tourism Zone spans vast tracts of pristine tropical rainforest near Sanya, offering one of Hainan's most thrilling nature experiences. The park features a spectacular canopy walkway suspended 40 meters above the forest floor, glass-bottom bridges spanning deep gorges, and a 1,500-meter-long zipline soaring through the jungle canopy at exhilarating speeds. Natural swimming pools formed by crystal-clear mountain streams invite visitors to cool off in the tropical heat. Hidden within the rainforest are mysterious waterfall grottos, ancient banyan trees draped in hanging vines, and a rich diversity of butterflies and tropical birds.
